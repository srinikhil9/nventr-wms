Our mission: make robots trustworthy enough to operate in shared spaces

Fully autonomous robots aren't ready for complex warehouse environments where humans, goods, and vehicles intersect. cobots takes a different approach: layered supervision where AI handles routine operations and trained human operators step in for edge cases, anomalies, and safety-critical decisions.

Every robotic action flows through five control layers -- from on-device AI to human escalation. The result is a system that's fast enough for production, safe enough for regulators, and transparent enough for your operations team.

The cobots Edge

Why human-supervised beats fully autonomous

Autonomy is a spectrum, not a switch. Our layered approach earns trust incrementally while delivering value from day one.

01

Human override at every layer

From hardware kill-switches on the robot to one-click fidelity escalation in the dashboard, a human can always take control. RL policies run in advisory mode until proven safe.

02

Explainable anomaly detection

Every AI flag comes with an XAI summary explaining why it was flagged, not just a confidence score. Operators see context -- bounding boxes, temporal patterns, sensor correlations.

03

Privacy by design

Face and plate redaction happens at the edge -- before data ever leaves the warehouse. Differential privacy in analytics pipelines, data minimisation by default, and DSR-ready architecture.

04

Audit-grade traceability

Immutable, checksummed, append-only logs for every escalation, fidelity change, and control action. Full distributed tracing from device to cloud with OpenTelemetry.
